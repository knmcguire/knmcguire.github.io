## Selecting a coworking space

So when I decided to go freelancing a year ago, it was already clear to me that In eeded to find a space to work together with other people. I have heard of the Game habitat, and then is where I started looking and working first. First of all, a lot of our robotics simulators we use are inspired by exisiting game enginers. And also since I am a game geek (or so I thought) I had an interest wahat the game development industry was about.

What I was looking for are the connections between robotics enginereing and game development. Usually when you are in between discipline and are able to connect those towo togther, that is where you can discover new things. Ever wondered where behavior trees come from, now widely used in Robotics autonomy, that's right, game development.

## Need for modular design

So similarities of these fields are perhaps the push to modular design of either the robot of games. In game development, that is because games are usually developed by very big and diverse teams. There needs to be specaes where the technical artisst can work, and that the gam play and level desingers can easily move things around, and that the programmers are able to intergrate more tools and plugins. Game angines are the glue that bind all of these things together. 

This interaction make me thinkg a a lot about the true orgiginal purpose was of ROS, the robotics operating system. Perhaps robotics teams are smaller than game development industries, the focus is on reproducability of the contributed open source packages between accademic and research groups when ROS firs tstarted out. That makes it easier for others to use code and packages developed by other groups. Perhaps you can see that even that the teams are smaller, the global open-source community are bigger (so you can see it as one big team)

## Focus on the player

THe biggest difference I see though, is the interaction. IN robotics, the biggest focus is to design system that can accomplish a task as eficient and as fast as possible, with the human interaction plays a secondary role. If a robot is not secured byind a big cage with a red button, it needs to be safe for humans. Autonomous vehicloes in particular should stop for any syclist. But that is not what it was designed for as a main purpose, which is just to be able to go from A to B.

Almost all games are designed for the purpose to entertain the human. They have not been made to solely only test your GPU. All control input, autonomous NPCS, or leves are centered around the player. If it is not smooth, fast and most importantly, fun,, no wone will by the game and tath's it. Playtest sessions are therefore an mandatory requirement for any succesfull game, where play testsrs who are not part of the team or family, can play the game given as little (ideally none) instructions as possible. In industrial design engineering (what I can still remember), this is called User tests.

Oh yeah, and sound and music design is hardly a thing in the robotics industry,. but call me up if there is something like a robot foley artist!

## Future of game development

If you are a gamer yourself, is that the game development is going through a transition right now. Many lay-offs are happening, also here in Malmö, and that has been happening for a good few years now. I perhaps have joined the game habitat at a wrong time... however, at least they had a spot for me to join. And is it really a bad time to get into it?

Because of my tendency of being in between diciplines and being a constant outsider, I was acked to join the futur of game workshop core group organized by Media evolution here in Malmö. Here we tried to look beyoind the thunder clouds of current affairs and looked at a world where games are perhaps more intergrated part of our lives. Will the industry recover, become bigger, or perhaps more local and decentralized, who knows! Eitheway, it was quite nice to be in the same room with people who where both gaming fans, developers or worked on simialr fields, wishin for these wonderful things to continue.

## Becoming a game developer myself?

That is a thought that I walked around with for yars, especially when current work situations at the time became tough. I am definitely not trained to be one but looking at individual skillsets related to robotics engineering, there are here and there definitely parts that are transferable. Especially since I started out with industrial design so I understand the human centric and can do some graphic design myself.

However, to be able to make a full fledged game from the start to finish requireds dedication  vision and  focus that is on an whole other level. Perhaps things are easier with AI tooling, but it still requires the full knowlede of how a game comes together, namely the game deisng. I have tried some mall things here and there on different enginers, but I'm not sure if I'd call them games. What I defintiely have gotten is a new found respect for solo indie game developers. 


Basically, if I do wnat to become a game developer for my carrier, i think there is a whole bunch of courses that I would need to do, and I would need to really dedicate a whol chunk of my time (maybe a year) to deilver something even remotely layable. That means setting my rbotics engineering and the great open source community that comes asside, and I have the feeling that I just really got started with that! Which means that I'll need to tap into my personal time, but that means that I actually don't get to play games anymore!

## Games in Malmö

Perhaps I can leave the ambition to become a game developer itself, but this doesn't meetan that I could not be part of the game industry. Indie studieos are popping up like mushrooms here in Malmö, with games just waiting to be played! Perhaps instead of making games, I could simply play tiem and write articles about them here? In anyway, I'll be continueing to co-work at the game habitat and planning to also move with them with the same location. I really like working here and I feel that I'm learning a lot from the people around me, so I hope that I can be helping them out as well!
